1. The German Alphabet and Pronunciation (Das deutsche Alphabet und die Aussprache)

The German alphabet has 26 letters, just like English.

There are also four special characters:

ä (a-Umlaut): Pronounced like the 'e' in 'bed'.
ö (o-Umlaut): Pronounced like the 'ur' in 'burn' (British English) or a similar sound.
ü (u-Umlaut): Pronounced like the 'ew' in 'few'.
ß (Eszett or sharp s): Pronounced like a double 's'.
Key Pronunciation Points for Beginners:

v is usually pronounced like an 'f' (e.g., Vogel - bird).
w is pronounced like a 'v' (e.g., Wasser - water).
z is pronounced like a 'ts' (e.g., Zahl - number).
s is pronounced like a 'z' before a vowel (e.g., Sonne - sun).
ch after 'i' or 'e' is a soft 'sh' sound (e.g., ich - I, Milch - milk).
ch after 'a', 'o', 'u' is a guttural sound, like the 'ch' in Scottish 'loch' (e.g., Buch - book).
ie is pronounced like a long 'ee' (e.g., Liebe - love).
ei is pronounced like 'eye' (e.g., mein - my).
eu is pronounced like 'oy' (e.g., neu - new).
2. Basic Sentence Structure (Der einfache Satzbau)

The most common sentence structure in German is Subject - Verb - Object (SVO), similar to English.

Ich (Subject) bin (Verb) Anna. (Object/Complement) - I am Anna.
Du (Subject) sprichst (Verb) Deutsch. (Object) - You speak German.
Sometimes the object comes before other parts of the sentence.

3. Nouns and Articles (Die Nomen und Artikel)

Every noun in German has a grammatical gender:

Masculine (der): der Mann (the man)
Feminine (die): die Frau (the woman)
Neuter (das): das Kind (the child)
You need to learn the gender of each noun as it affects the articles and other parts of speech.

Definite Articles (Bestimmte Artikel): Used when you are talking about a specific person or thing.

| Gender    | Singular | Plural    |
| :-------- | :------- | :-------- |
| Masculine | der      | die (same) |
| Feminine  | die      | die (same) |
| Neuter    | das      | die (same) |

Der Tisch (the table)
Die Lampe (the lamp)
Das Buch (the book)
Die Tische (the tables)
Die Lampen (the lamps)
Die Bücher (the books)
Indefinite Articles (Unbestimmte Artikel): Used when you are talking about a general or non-specific person or thing.

| Gender    | Singular | Plural |
| :-------- | :------- | :----- |
| Masculine | ein      | keine  |
| Feminine  | eine     | keine  |
| Neuter    | ein      | keine  |

Ein Mann (a man)
Eine Frau (a woman)
Ein Kind (a child)

Keine Frauen (no women)
Keine Kinder (no children)
4. Verbs (Die Verben)

Verbs in German are conjugated, meaning their form changes depending on the subject.

Present Tense Conjugation of Regular Verbs: The basic pattern involves dropping the -en ending from the infinitive and adding specific endings.

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: machen (to make) | Example: spielen (to play) |
| :------ | :----- | :------------------------- | :-------------------------- |
| ich     | -e     | ich mache | ich spiele |
| du      | -st    | du machst | du spielst |
| er/sie/es | -t     | er/sie/es macht | er/sie/es spielt |
| wir     | -en    | wir machen | wir spielen |
| ihr     | -t     | ihr macht | ihr spielt |
| sie/Sie | -en    | sie/Sie machen | sie/Sie spielen |

Sie (lowercase) means "they".
Sie (uppercase) means "you" (formal).

5. Personal Pronouns (Die Personalpronomen)

Pronoun	English	Example
ich	I	Ich wohne in Berlin. (I live in Berlin.)
du	you (informal)	Du bist mein Freund. (You are my friend.)
er	he	Er kommt aus Deutschland. (He comes from Germany.)
sie	she	Sie ist Lehrerin. (She is a teacher.)
es	it	Es ist kalt heute. (It is cold today.)
wir	we	Wir lernen Deutsch. (We are learning German.)
ihr	you (plural, informal)	Ihr seid nett. (You are nice.)
sie	they	Sie spielen Fußball. (They play football.)
Sie	you (formal)	Sie sprechen gut Deutsch. (You speak German well.)

6. Basic Adjectives (Die einfachen Adjektive)

Adjectives describe nouns. In their basic form (before a noun), they don't usually change in A1 level.

ein kleines Haus (a small house) - Note: Here the adjective ending changes, but this is usually introduced later. For A1, focus on the basic form.
eine gute Idee (a good idea)
der nette Mann (the nice man)
Common A1 Adjectives:

gut (good)
schlecht (bad)
groß (big)
klein (small)
neu (new)
alt (old)
schön (beautiful)
nett (nice)

8. Basic Questions (Die einfachen Fragen)

Yes/No Questions (Ja/Nein-Fragen): The verb comes at the beginning of the sentence.

Bist du müde? (Are you tired?)
Sprechen Sie Englisch? (Do you speak English?)
W-Questions (W-Fragen): These questions start with a question word (Wort).

| Question Word | English | Example                               |
| :------------ | :------ | :------------------------------------ |
| wer           | who     | Wer sind Sie? (Who are you?)     |
| was           | what    | Was ist das? (What is that?)      |
| wann          | when    | Wann kommst du? (When are you coming?) |
| wo            | where   | Wo wohnst du? (Where do you live?) |
| wohin         | where to | Wohin gehst du? (Where are you going?) |
| woher         | where from | Woher kommen Sie? (Where do you come from?) |
| wie           | how     | Wie geht es Ihnen? (How are you?) (formal) |
| wie viel      | how much | Wie viel kostet das? (How much does that cost?) |

9. Basic Prepositions (Die einfachen Präpositionen)

Prepositions show the relationship between nouns or pronouns and other words in the sentence (e.g., location, time).

Prepositions of Place:

in (in) - Ich wohne in Berlin. (I live in Berlin.)
auf (on) - Das Buch liegt auf dem Tisch. (The book is on the table.)
unter (under) - Die Katze ist unter dem Stuhl. (The cat is under the chair.)
neben (next to) - Das Kino ist neben dem Supermarkt. (The cinema is next to the supermarket.)
vor (in front of) - Das Auto steht vor dem Haus. (The car is in front of the house.)
hinter (behind) - Der Garten ist hinter dem Haus. (The garden is behind the house.)
zwischen (between) - Die Bank ist zwischen dem Baum und dem Fluss. (The bench is between the tree and the river.)
Prepositions of Time:

am (on - for days of the week and parts of the day) - Ich gehe am Montag ins Kino. (I go to the cinema on Monday.) / Ich stehe am Morgen auf. (I get up in the morning.)
im (in - for months and seasons) - Wir fahren im Sommer in Urlaub. (We go on vacation in the summer.) / Ich bin im Juli geboren. (I was born in July.)
um (at - for specific times) - Der Unterricht beginnt um 9 Uhr. (The lesson starts at 9 o'clock.)
