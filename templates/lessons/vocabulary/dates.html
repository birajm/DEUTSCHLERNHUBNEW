Days and Months

Learn days of the week and months of the year in German

Overview

Being able to talk about days, months, and dates is essential for everyday conversations in German. In this lesson, you'll learn the names of days of the week, months of the year, and seasons, as well as how to ask and answer questions about dates.

Note that in German:

  • All days of the week and months of the year are capitalized (like all nouns in German).
  • Days of the week are masculine (der).
  • Months of the year are also masculine (der).

Days of the Week (die Wochentage)

Here are the days of the week in German:

German English Pronunciation Listen
Montag Monday MON-tahk
Dienstag Tuesday DEENS-tahk
Mittwoch Wednesday MIT-vokh
Donnerstag Thursday DON-ers-tahk
Freitag Friday FRY-tahk
Samstag/Sonnabend* Saturday ZAMS-tahk/ZON-ah-bent
Sonntag Sunday ZON-tahk
Note:
  • "Tag" means "day" in German. You can see this ending in most of the days of the week.
  • * "Sonnabend" is an alternative word for Saturday used mainly in northern and eastern Germany.
  • The German week starts with Monday (Montag) as the first day.
Useful Phrases with Days
  • Heute ist Montag. - Today is Monday.
  • Morgen ist Dienstag. - Tomorrow is Tuesday.
  • Gestern war Sonntag. - Yesterday was Sunday.
  • Am Mittwoch habe ich Deutschunterricht. - On Wednesday I have German class.
  • Ich arbeite von Montag bis Freitag. - I work from Monday to Friday.
  • Das Geschäft ist am Sonntag geschlossen. - The store is closed on Sunday.

Useful Phrases with Months
  • Wir sind jetzt im Mai. - We are now in May.
  • Mein Geburtstag ist im Juli. - My birthday is in July.
  • Im Dezember ist Weihnachten. - Christmas is in December.
  • Die Schule beginnt im September. - School starts in September.

Seasons (die Jahreszeiten)

Here are the four seasons in German:

Spring (der Frühling)

German: der Frühling or das Frühjahr

Pronunciation: FROO-ling / FROO-yar

Months: März, April, Mai

Summer (der Sommer)

German: der Sommer

Pronunciation: ZOM-mer

Months: Juni, Juli, August

Autumn/Fall (der Herbst)

German: der Herbst

Pronunciation: HERPST

Months: September, Oktober, November

Winter (der Winter)

German: der Winter

Pronunciation: VIN-ter

Months: Dezember, Januar, Februar

Useful Phrases with Seasons
  • Im Sommer ist es warm. - It's warm in the summer.
  • Der Frühling beginnt im März. - Spring begins in March.
  • Im Herbst fallen die Blätter von den Bäumen. - In autumn, the leaves fall from the trees.
  • Es schneit oft im Winter. - It often snows in winter.
  • Meine Lieblingsjahreszeit ist der Sommer. - My favorite season is summer.

Asking about Dates

Here are some useful phrases and questions related to dates in German:

Questions
  • Welcher Tag ist heute? - What day is today?
  • Welches Datum haben wir heute? - What's today's date?
  • Welcher Monat ist jetzt? - What month is it now?
  • In welchem Monat hast du Geburtstag? - In which month is your birthday?
  • Wann ist das Konzert? - When is the concert?
Answers
  • Heute ist Montag. - Today is Monday.
  • Heute ist der 15. Mai. - Today is May 15th.
  • Jetzt ist April. - It's April now.
  • Ich habe im September Geburtstag. - My birthday is in September.
  • Das Konzert ist am Freitag, dem 20. Juli. - The concert is on Friday, July 20th.
Grammar Note:
  • When talking about specific dates, use "am" (on) + the day: am Montag (on Monday).
  • When talking about months, use "im" (in) + the month: im Januar (in January).
  • To express dates, the format in German is: den [number]. [month] or am [number]. [month].
  • Example: den 23. April or am 23. April (April 23rd).
  • Note the period after the number, which indicates an ordinal number (23rd, not just 23).
