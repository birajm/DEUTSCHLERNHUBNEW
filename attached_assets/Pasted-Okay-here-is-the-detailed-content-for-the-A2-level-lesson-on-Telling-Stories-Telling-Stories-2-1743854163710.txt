Okay, here is the detailed content for the A2 level lesson on Telling Stories:

**Telling Stories (25 min):**

* **Introduction:** Explain that being able to tell simple stories is a fun and important part of learning a language. In this lesson, you will learn some useful vocabulary and phrases to narrate past events in German. We will mainly focus on using the *Perfekt* tense, which you have already learned.

* **Key Vocabulary for Telling Stories:**

    * **Time Markers:**
        * *gestern* (yesterday)
        * *vorgestern* (the day before yesterday)
        * *letzte Woche* (last week)
        * *letzten Monat* (last month)
        * *letztes Jahr* (last year)
        * *früher* (earlier, in the past)
        * *eines Tages* (one day)
        * *plötzlich* (suddenly)
        * *dann* (then)
        * *später* (later)
        * *danach* (after that)
        * *endlich* (finally)
    * **Connecting Words:**
        * *und* (and)
        * *aber* (but)
        * *oder* (or)
        * *denn* (because)
        * *deshalb* (therefore)
        * *weil* (because)
    * **Other Useful Words:**
        * *passieren* (to happen)
        * *erzählen* (to tell)
        * *fragen* (to ask)
        * *antworten* (to answer)
        * *lachen* (to laugh)
        * *weinen* (to cry)
        * *sagen* (to say)
        * *denken* (to think)
        * *finden* (to find)
        * *sehen* (to see)
        * *hören* (to hear)

* **Grammar Focus: The Perfekt Tense:**

    * Remind learners that the *Perfekt* tense is formed with the auxiliary verb *haben* or *sein* and the *Partizip II* of the main verb.
    * Briefly review when to use *haben* and when to use *sein*.
    * Encourage learners to use the *Perfekt* tense when telling their stories in this lesson.

* **Useful Phrases for Starting, Connecting, and Ending a Story:**

    * **Starting a Story:**
        * *Gestern habe ich...* (Yesterday I...)
        * *Letzte Woche ist etwas Interessantes passiert.* (Last week something interesting happened.)
        * *Eines Tages habe ich...* (One day I...)
        * *Weißt du, was gestern passiert ist?* (Do you know what happened yesterday?)
    * **Connecting Events in a Story:**
        * *Und dann...* (And then...)
        * *Danach habe ich...* (After that, I...)
        * *Plötzlich...* (Suddenly...)
        * *Aber...* (But...)
        * *Weil...* (Because...)
        * *Deshalb habe ich...* (Therefore, I...)
    * **Ending a Story:**
        * *Und am Ende...* (And in the end...)
        * *Das war's.* (That was it.)
        * *So ist das passiert.* (That's how it happened.)
        * *Das war eine interessante Erfahrung.* (That was an interesting experience.)

* **Example Story:**

    *Read the following simple story aloud or display it on the screen:*

    > Gestern bin ich in den Park gegangen. Dort habe ich einen alten Freund getroffen. Wir haben uns lange nicht gesehen. Er hat mir von seiner neuen Arbeit erzählt. Dann haben wir zusammen Kaffee getrunken. Plötzlich hat es angefangen zu regnen. Deshalb sind wir schnell nach Hause gegangen. Das war ein schöner Tag.

    *Translate the story for learners if needed and highlight the use of the *Perfekt* tense and the connecting words.*

* **Practice Activities:**

    1.  **Fill in the Blanks:** Provide a short story with missing words (from the "Key Vocabulary" section) and ask learners to fill in the blanks.

        *Example:* _______ bin ich ins Kino _______. Dort habe ich einen _______ Film _______. _______ habe ich Popcorn _______. _______ ist der Film zu Ende _______.

        *Possible Answers:* Gestern, gegangen, interessanten, gesehen, Dann, gegessen, Plötzlich, gewesen.

    2.  **Picture Story:** Show a sequence of 3-4 simple pictures and ask learners to tell a short story based on the pictures using the learned vocabulary and phrases. Encourage them to use the *Perfekt* tense.

        *Example Pictures:*
            * A person waking up late.
            * The person running to catch a bus.
            * The person arriving at work looking stressed.

        *Possible Story Starters:* *Gestern bin ich spät aufgewacht... Dann bin ich schnell zum Bus gerannt...*

    3.  **Tell Your Own Story:** Ask learners to think about something simple that happened to them yesterday or last week and tell a short story about it using at least three of the "Key Vocabulary" words and the *Perfekt* tense. They can write it down or tell it to a partner (if applicable in the learning environment).

    4.  **Ordering Events:** Provide a few sentences from a short story in the wrong order and ask learners to put them in the correct sequence.

        *Example Sentences:*
            * Dann habe ich ein Eis gegessen.
            * Zuerst bin ich in den Supermarkt gegangen.
            * Am Ende bin ich glücklich nach Hause gegangen.
            * Ich habe Obst und Gemüse gekauft.

        *Correct Order:* Zuerst bin ich in den Supermarkt gegangen. Ich habe Obst und Gemüse gekauft. Dann habe ich ein Eis gegessen. Am Ende bin ich glücklich nach Hause gegangen.

* **Conclusion:** Briefly summarize what learners have learned in this lesson and encourage them to practice telling more stories in German.

Remember to include audio pronunciations for the vocabulary words and example sentences to aid learning. You can also add visual elements like pictures to make the lesson more engaging.
