Okay, here is the detailed content for the A2 level grammar lessons on Past Tense (Perfekt), Modal Verbs, and Prepositions:

**Grammar:**

* **Past Tense (Perfekt):**
    * **Introduction:** Explain that the *Perfekt* tense is a common way to talk about the past in spoken German. It's formed with a helping verb (*haben* or *sein*) and the *Partizip II* (past participle) of the main verb.
    * **Auxiliary Verbs: Haben and Sein:**
        * Explain when to use *haben* and when to use *sein* as the helping verb.
        * Generally, *haben* is used with transitive verbs (verbs that take a direct object) and most intransitive verbs (verbs that don't take a direct object).
        * *Sein* is used with intransitive verbs that express a change in location or state (e.g., *gehen*, *kommen*, *bleiben*, *werden*, *sein*).
        * Provide examples:
            * *Ich **habe** ein Buch **gelesen**.* (I read a book.) - *lesen* is transitive, so *haben*.
            * *Wir **haben** Fußball **gespielt**.* (We played football.) - *spielen* is transitive, so *haben*.
            * *Er **ist** nach Hause **gegangen**.* (He went home.) - *gehen* expresses a change in location, so *sein*.
            * *Sie **ist** müde **geworden**.* (She became tired.) - *werden* expresses a change in state, so *sein*.
            * *Ich **bin** zu Hause **geblieben**.* (I stayed at home.) - *bleiben* expresses a state of being in a location, so *sein*.
    * **Formation of Partizip II:**
        * Explain the basic rules for forming the *Partizip II*:
            * For regular verbs: **ge-** + verb stem + **-t**
                * *spielen* -> *gespielt*
                * *kaufen* -> *gekauft*
                * *machen* -> *gemacht*
            * For irregular verbs: **ge-** + changed verb stem + **-en** (often with a vowel change in the stem)
                * *lesen* -> *gelesen*
                * *schreiben* -> *geschrieben*
                * *essen* -> *gegessen*
                * *fahren* -> *gefahren*
            * Verbs ending in *-ieren* do not usually take *ge-* in the *Partizip II*:
                * *studieren* -> *studiert*
                * *telefonieren* -> *telefoniert*
        * Provide a list of common irregular verbs and their *Partizip II* forms.
    * **Word Order in Perfekt Sentences:**
        * Explain that in a main clause, the conjugated auxiliary verb (*haben* or *sein*) comes in the second position, and the *Partizip II* goes to the end of the sentence.
        * Provide examples:
            * *Ich **habe** gestern ein Eis **gegessen**.* (I ate an ice cream yesterday.)
            * *Sie **ist** am Wochenende nach Berlin **gefahren**.* (She went to Berlin on the weekend.)
            * *Wir **haben** viele Freunde **getroffen**.* (We met many friends.)
    * **Practice Activities:**
        * Filling in the correct form of *haben* or *sein*.
        * Forming the *Partizip II* of given verbs.
        * Constructing sentences in the *Perfekt* tense.
        * Translating simple past tense sentences into German using the *Perfekt*.

* **Modal Verbs:**
    * **Introduction:** Introduce the six main modal verbs in German: *können* (can, to be able to), *müssen* (must, to have to), *dürfen* (may, to be allowed to), *sollen* (should, ought to), *wollen* (want to), *möchten* (would like to).
    * **Meanings and Usage:**
        * Explain the basic meaning of each modal verb and common situations in which they are used.
            * ***können***: ability, possibility (*Ich kann schwimmen.* - I can swim.)
            * ***müssen***: necessity, obligation (*Du musst deine Hausaufgaben machen.* - You must do your homework.)
            * ***dürfen***: permission, prohibition (*Hier dürfen Sie nicht rauchen.* - You are not allowed to smoke here.)
            * ***sollen***: obligation (often from someone else), advice (*Du sollst mehr Obst essen.* - You should eat more fruit.)
            * ***wollen***: strong desire, intention (*Ich will ein neues Auto kaufen.* - I want to buy a new car.)
            * ***möchten***: polite request, desire (*Ich möchte einen Kaffee bestellen.* - I would like to order a coffee.) (Note: *möchten* is the Konjunktiv II form of *mögen* and is generally considered more polite than *wollen*.)
    * **Conjugation of Modal Verbs:**
        * Provide the conjugation tables for each modal verb in the present tense, highlighting the fact that the 1st and 3rd person singular forms have no ending.
    * **Word Order with Modal Verbs:**
        * Explain that when a modal verb is used in a sentence, it is conjugated and placed in the second position. The main verb goes to the end of the sentence in its infinitive form.
        * Provide examples:
            * *Ich **kann** gut Deutsch **sprechen**.* (I can speak German well.)
            * *Du **musst** jetzt **gehen**.* (You have to go now.)
            * *Wir **dürfen** hier nicht **parken**.* (We are not allowed to park here.)
            * *Er **soll** seine Mutter **anrufen**.* (He should call his mother.)
            * *Sie **will** ein Buch **lesen**.* (She wants to read a book.)
            * *Ich **möchte** ein Wasser **bestellen**.* (I would like to order a water.)
    * **Practice Activities:**
        * Filling in the correct modal verb in sentences.
        * Choosing the correct form of the modal verb.
        * Constructing sentences using modal verbs and infinitives.
        * Translating sentences with modal verbs into German.

* **Prepositions:**
    * **Introduction:** Explain that prepositions are words that show the relationship between a noun or pronoun and other words in the sentence. In German, prepositions often determine the case of the noun or pronoun that follows them.
    * **Prepositions with Accusative Case:**
        * List common A2 level accusative prepositions: *durch* (through), *für* (for), *gegen* (against), *ohne* (without), *um* (around - time).
        * Provide example sentences for each:
            * *Der Zug fährt **durch** den Tunnel.* (The train goes through the tunnel.)
            * *Dieses Geschenk ist **für** dich.* (This present is for you.)
            * *Wir spielen **gegen** die andere Mannschaft.* (We are playing against the other team.)
            * *Ich trinke Kaffee **ohne** Zucker.* (I drink coffee without sugar.)
            * *Der Unterricht beginnt **um** 9 Uhr.* (The lesson starts at 9 o'clock.)
    * **Prepositions with Dative Case:**
        * List common A2 level dative prepositions: *aus* (from, out of), *bei* (at, near, with), *mit* (with), *nach* (after - time, to - cities/countries without article), *seit* (since), *von* (from, of), *zu* (to - people/places within a city).
        * Provide example sentences for each:
            * *Sie kommt **aus** der Schweiz.* (She comes from Switzerland.)
            * *Er wohnt **bei** seiner Familie.* (He lives with his family.)
            * *Ich fahre **mit** dem Fahrrad.* (I go by bicycle.)
            * ***Nach** der Arbeit gehe ich nach Hause.* (After work, I go home.)
            * ***Nach** Spanien möchte ich auch mal reisen.* (I would also like to travel to Spain sometime.)
            * *Ich wohne hier **seit** drei Monaten.* (I have been living here for three months.)
            * *Das ist ein Brief **von** meinem Bruder.* (That is a letter from my brother.)
            * *Ich gehe **zum** Arzt.* (I am going to the doctor.)
            * *Wir fahren **nach** Hause.* (We are going home.)
    * **Practice Activities:**
        * Filling in the correct preposition in sentences.
        * Choosing the correct case (accusative or dative) for the noun following a preposition.
        * Matching prepositions to their meanings.
        * Translating simple sentences with prepositions into German.
