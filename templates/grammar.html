Nouns and Gender

Gender Rules

In German, every noun has a gender: masculine, feminine, or neuter. While there are no absolute rules, there are some patterns to help guess the gender:

Masculine Nouns (der)

  • Days, months, and seasons
  • Most nouns ending in -er when referring to male people
  • Many nouns ending in -en, -el, -ling, -ismus
  • Examples: der Tag (day), der Sommer (summer), der Lehrer (teacher)

Feminine Nouns (die)

  • Most nouns ending in -e
  • Nouns ending in -heit, -keit, -ung, -schaft, -tion
  • Many nouns ending in -ie, -ik, -ur
  • Examples: die Frau (woman), die Freiheit (freedom), die Nation (nation)

Neuter Nouns (das)

  • Diminutives ending in -chen or -lein
  • Most metals and chemical elements
  • Most infinitives used as nouns
  • Examples: das Kind (child), das Gold (gold), das Essen (food/eating)

Plural Formation

There are several ways to form plurals in German:

Pattern Singular Plural
Add -e der Tag die Tage
Add -er (with possible umlaut) das Kind die Kinder
Add -n or -en die Frau die Frauen
Add -s (foreign words) das Auto die Autos
Same form der Lehrer die Lehrer

Cases

The Four German Cases

Nominative Case

Used for the subject of the sentence.

Question: Who/what is doing the action?

Der Mann kauft ein Buch.

The man buys a book.

Accusative Case

Used for the direct object of the sentence.

Question: Who/what is receiving the action?

Der Mann kauft ein Buch.

The man buys a book.

Dative Case

Used for the indirect object of the sentence.

Question: To/for whom is the action done?

Er gibt dem Kind ein Geschenk.

He gives the child a gift.

Genitive Case

Used to show possession or relationships.

Question: Whose?

Das Fahrrad des Mannes ist neu.

The man's bicycle is new.

Verbs and Conjugation

Regular Verbs in Present Tense

Example: spielen (to play)

Person Conjugation Example
ich (I) stem + e ich spiele
du (you, informal) stem + st du spielst
er/sie/es (he/she/it) stem + t er/sie/es spielt
wir (we) stem + en wir spielen
ihr (you all, informal) stem + t ihr spielt
sie/Sie (they/you formal) stem + en sie/Sie spielen
