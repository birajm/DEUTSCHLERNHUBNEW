**Advanced Communication:**

* **Expressing Complex Ideas (30 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Explain the need for more nuanced language at the B1 level.
    * **Expressing Opinions with Justification:** Teach phrases like "Ich bin der Meinung, dass...", "Ich glaube, weil...", "Meiner Ansicht nach...", "Dafür spricht, dass...".
    * **Expressing Agreement and Disagreement with Nuance:** "Ich stimme Ihnen/dir im Prinzip zu, aber...", "Ich sehe das etwas anders, da...", "Einerseits..., andererseits...".
    * **Formulating Hypotheses:** Introduce the *Konjunktiv II* (will be covered in grammar) for hypothetical situations: "Ich würde sagen...", "Wenn ich könnte, würde ich...".
    * **Expressing Abstract Concepts:** Provide vocabulary and phrases for discussing abstract ideas like freedom, justice, equality, progress.
    * **Using Connectors to Link Ideas:** Introduce more advanced conjunctions and adverbs like "außerdem" (furthermore), "deshalb" (therefore), "trotzdem" (nevertheless), "hingegen" (on the other hand).
    * **Practice Activities:** Discussing complex topics in small groups, writing short paragraphs expressing opinions with reasons, completing sentences with appropriate connecting words.

* **Describing Experiences (25 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Focus on using different tenses effectively to narrate past events.
    * **Using *Perfekt* and *Präteritum*:** Explain when to use each tense for narration (e.g., *Perfekt* for recent events, *Präteritum* for more formal storytelling and in written texts). Provide examples of using both tenses within a narrative.
    * **Adding Details and Adjectives:** Encourage the use of descriptive adjectives and adverbs to make stories more engaging.
    * **Sequencing Events:** Introduce more sophisticated sequencing words and phrases like "zuerst," "danach," "anschließend," "währenddessen" (meanwhile), "bevor" (before), "nachdem" (after).
    * **Expressing Feelings and Reactions:** Teach vocabulary for describing emotions and reactions to events.
    * **Practice Activities:** Telling stories based on prompts or pictures, writing short narratives about past experiences, role-playing recounting an event to someone.

* **Debating and Persuading (35 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Explain the basics of argumentation and debate.
    * **Stating Your Position Clearly:** "Ich bin davon überzeugt, dass...", "Ich vertrete den Standpunkt, dass...".
    * **Providing Arguments and Evidence:** "Ein wichtiger Punkt ist...", "Das lässt sich dadurch belegen, dass...", "Zum Beispiel...".
    * **Formulating Counter-Arguments:** "Man könnte einwenden, dass...", "Demgegenüber steht jedoch, dass...", "Einige Leute argumentieren, dass...".
    * **Persuading Others:** "Ich möchte Sie davon überzeugen, dass...", "Es wäre vorteilhaft, wenn...", "Denken Sie nur daran, dass...".
    * **Using Rhetorical Questions:** Introduce the use of rhetorical questions to engage the audience.
    * **Practice Activities:** Participating in short debates on given topics, writing argumentative essays, preparing and presenting arguments for a specific viewpoint.

* **Cultural Nuances (25 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Highlight the importance of understanding cultural context in communication.
    * **Common Idiomatic Expressions:** Introduce and explain the meaning of common German idioms (e.g., "jemandem die Daumen drücken" - to keep one's fingers crossed for someone, "ins Fettnäpfchen treten" - to put one's foot in one's mouth).
    * **Understanding Implicit Communication:** Discuss how Germans might communicate indirectly or rely on shared cultural understanding.
    * **Social Etiquette:** Cover aspects of German social etiquette, such as punctuality, greetings, and gift-giving.
    * **Cultural References:** Introduce basic knowledge of German history, geography, and famous cultural figures.
    * **Practice Activities:** Discussing the meaning of idioms in context, analyzing short dialogues for implicit communication, researching and presenting on aspects of German culture.

**Grammar:**

* **Passive Voice (30 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Explain the purpose of the passive voice (focus on the action rather than the actor).
    * **Formation of *Vorgangspassiv* (Process Passive):** Explain the use of the auxiliary verb *werden* and the past participle. Show conjugation of *werden* in different tenses (present, past). Provide examples: "Das Auto wird repariert." (The car is being repaired.), "Das Fenster wurde geöffnet." (The window was opened.).
    * **Formation of *Zustandspassiv* (Statal Passive):** Explain the use of the auxiliary verb *sein* and the past participle (often indicating a resulting state). Provide examples: "Die Tür ist geschlossen." (The door is closed.).
    * **Using Agents in Passive Sentences:** Explain how to indicate the actor using "von + Dativ."
    * **Passive Voice with Modal Verbs:** Show how modal verbs are used in passive constructions.
    * **Practice Activities:** Transforming active sentences into passive sentences, filling in the blanks in passive sentences, identifying the type of passive voice used.

* **Subjunctive (Konjunktiv II) (35 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Explain the different uses of the *Konjunktiv II*: hypothetical situations, wishes, polite requests, and indirect speech.
    * **Formation of the *Konjunktiv II* Forms of *haben*, *sein*, and Modal Verbs:** Show the specific *Konjunktiv II* forms.
    * **Formation of the *Konjunktiv II* of Regular and Irregular Verbs:** Explain the stem changes (often with umlauts) and the endings. Introduce the use of *würde + Infinitiv* as an alternative for many verbs.
    * **Expressing Hypothetical Situations:** "Wenn ich Zeit hätte, würde ich reisen." (If I had time, I would travel.).
    * **Expressing Wishes:** "Ich wünschte, ich wäre reich." (I wish I were rich.).
    * **Making Polite Requests:** "Könnten Sie mir bitte helfen?" (Could you please help me?).
    * **Indirect Speech:** Explain how to report what someone else said using *Konjunktiv II*.
    * **Practice Activities:** Transforming sentences into the *Konjunktiv II*, completing sentences with the correct *Konjunktiv II* form, translating sentences expressing hypothetical situations or wishes.

* **Advanced Tenses (30 min):**
    * **Past Perfect (*Plusquamperfekt*):** Explain its use for actions that happened before another past action. Formation with *hatte* or *war* + past participle. Examples: "Nachdem ich gegessen hatte, ging ich spazieren." (After I had eaten, I went for a walk.).
    * **Future I (*Futur I*):** Explain its use for expressing future plans and predictions. Formation with *werden* + infinitive. Examples: "Ich werde morgen anrufen." (I will call tomorrow.), "Es wird regnen." (It will rain.).
    * **Future II (*Futur II*):** Explain its use for expressing actions that will be completed by a certain point in the future or for making assumptions about the past. Formation with *werden* + *sein/haben* + past participle. Examples: "Bis morgen werde ich die Arbeit erledigt haben." (By tomorrow I will have finished the work.), "Er wird wohl zu Hause gewesen sein." (He will probably have been at home.).
    * **Practice Activities:** Forming sentences in the past perfect and future tenses, translating sentences with advanced tenses, putting events in chronological order using different past tenses.

* **Complex Sentence Structures (35 min):**
    * **Relative Clauses:** Explain how to use relative pronouns (*der*, *die*, *das*, *welcher*, *welche*, *welches*) to add more information to nouns. Explain the word order in relative clauses (verb at the end). Examples: "Das ist das Buch, das ich gestern gekauft habe." (That is the book that I bought yesterday.).
    * **Indirect Questions (Indirekte Fragen):** Explain how to report questions using conjunctions like *ob* (whether) and question words. Explain the word order (verb at the end). Examples: "Ich frage mich, ob er kommt." (I wonder if he is coming.), "Sie fragte, wann der Zug fährt." (She asked when the train leaves.).
    * **Sentences with *um...zu*, *ohne...zu*, and *anstatt...zu*:** Explain how to use these constructions to express purpose, absence of something, and substitution. Examples: "Ich lerne Deutsch, um in Deutschland zu studieren." (I am learning German to study in Germany.), "Er ging weg, ohne etwas zu sagen." (He left without saying anything.).
    * **Sentences with Multiple Subordinate Clauses:** Show how to combine several subordinate clauses in one sentence and explain the resulting word order.
    * **Practice Activities:** Combining sentences using relative clauses, transforming direct questions into indirect questions, forming sentences using *um...zu* etc., analyzing complex sentences and identifying the different clauses.
