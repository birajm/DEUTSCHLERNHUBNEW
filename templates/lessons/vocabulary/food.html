Overview

Food and drink vocabulary is essential for everyday life in German-speaking countries. In this lesson, you'll learn basic food items, drinks, meal names, and useful phrases for ordering at restaurants.

As with all German nouns, food and drink terms have grammatical gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter), which determines the article used with them (der, die, das). It's important to learn each noun with its correct article.

Note:

In German, food nouns used in a general sense often don't require an article:

  • Ich esse Brot. (I'm eating bread.) - No article needed when speaking generally
  • Das Brot ist frisch. (The bread is fresh.) - Article used when referring to specific bread

Asking for Drinks
  • Ich hätte gerne... - I would like...
  • Kann ich bitte... haben? - Can I have... please?
  • Ich nehme... - I'll take...
  • Mit oder ohne Eis? - With or without ice?
  • Noch ein Glas, bitte. - Another glass, please.

Meals (Mahlzeiten)

Here are the main meals of the day in German:

das Frühstück
Breakfast

The first meal of the day, typically eaten in the morning.

Example: Zum Frühstück esse ich Müsli mit Joghurt.

(For breakfast, I eat muesli with yogurt.)

das Mittagessen
Lunch

The meal eaten in the middle of the day, typically around noon.

Example: Zum Mittagessen gehe ich in die Kantine.

(For lunch, I go to the cafeteria.)

das Abendessen
Dinner

The evening meal, typically the main meal of the day in many cultures.

Example: Zum Abendessen essen wir Brot mit Käse und Wurst.

(For dinner, we eat bread with cheese and sausage.)

Other meal-related terms:
  • die Vorspeise - appetizer/starter
  • die Hauptspeise - main course
  • der Nachtisch / das Dessert - dessert
  • der Snack / der Imbiss - snack
  • die Jause / die Brotzeit - mid-morning or afternoon snack (regional terms)

At the Restaurant (Im Restaurant)

Here are useful phrases for ordering food and eating out:

Common Restaurant Phrases
German English
Einen Tisch für zwei, bitte. A table for two, please.
Haben Sie reserviert? Have you made a reservation?
Die Speisekarte, bitte. The menu, please.
Ich möchte bestellen. I'd like to order.
Was können Sie empfehlen? What can you recommend?
Ich nehme... I'll take/have...
Ich bin Vegetarier/in. I am a vegetarian.
Ich habe eine Allergie gegen... I have an allergy to...
Die Rechnung, bitte. The bill, please.
Zusammen oder getrennt? Together or separate? (asking about the bill)
Stimmt so. Keep the change.
Restaurant Vocabulary
  • das Restaurant - restaurant
  • das Café - café
  • die Speisekarte - menu
  • der Kellner / die Kellnerin - waiter / waitress
  • das Trinkgeld - tip
  • die Suppe - soup
  • der Salat - salad
  • das Getränk - drink/beverage
  • das Besteck - cutlery
  • der Teller - plate
