A1 grammar forms the foundation of your German language learning. Master these concepts and you'll be well on your way to speaking German!

The German Alphabet and Pronunciation

The German alphabet has 26 letters, just like English, plus four special characters:

Special Characters
  • ä (a-Umlaut): Pronounced like the 'e' in 'bed'
  • ö (o-Umlaut): Pronounced like the 'ur' in 'burn' (British English)
  • ü (u-Umlaut): Pronounced like the 'ew' in 'few'
  • ß (Eszett or sharp s): Pronounced like a double 's'
Key Pronunciation Points
  • v is usually pronounced like an 'f' (e.g., Vogel - bird)
  • w is pronounced like a 'v' (e.g., Wasser - water)
  • z is pronounced like a 'ts' (e.g., Zahl - number)
  • s is pronounced like a 'z' before a vowel (e.g., Sonne - sun)

Vowel Combinations

Combination Pronunciation Example
ie Long 'ee' sound Liebe (love)
ei Like 'eye' mein (my)
eu Like 'oy' neu (new)
ch after i/e Soft 'sh' sound ich (I)
ch after a/o/u Guttural sound (like Scottish 'loch') Buch (book)

Basic Sentence Structure

The most common sentence structure in German is Subject - Verb - Object (SVO), similar to English.

Examples

Ich (Subject) bin (Verb) Anna (Complement).
I am Anna.

Du (Subject) sprichst (Verb) Deutsch (Object).
You speak German.

Important Rule: Verb Position

In German, the conjugated verb always comes in the second position in a main clause (except in questions and commands).

Examples

Ich gehe morgen ins Kino.
I am going to the cinema tomorrow.

Morgen gehe ich ins Kino.
Tomorrow I am going to the cinema.

Ins Kino gehe ich morgen.
To the cinema I am going tomorrow.

Nouns and Articles

Every noun in German has a grammatical gender (masculine, feminine, or neuter) that determines which article to use.

Grammatical Gender

Masculine (der)
  • der Mann (the man)
  • der Tisch (the table)
  • der Computer (the computer)
Feminine (die)
  • die Frau (the woman)
  • die Lampe (the lamp)
  • die Schule (the school)
Neuter (das)
  • das Kind (the child)
  • das Buch (the book)
  • das Auto (the car)
You need to learn the gender of each noun as it affects the articles and other parts of speech.

Definite Articles (Bestimmte Artikel)

Used when you are talking about a specific person or thing.

Gender Singular Plural
Masculine der die
Feminine die
Neuter das

Indefinite Articles (Unbestimmte Artikel)

Used when you are talking about a general or non-specific person or thing.

Gender Singular Plural
Masculine ein keine (means "no")
Feminine eine
Neuter ein
Examples

Der Tisch ist groß. (The table is big.)

Die Lampe ist neu. (The lamp is new.)

Das Buch ist interessant. (The book is interesting.)

Ein Mann steht dort. (A man is standing there.)

Eine Frau liest. (A woman is reading.)

Ein Kind spielt. (A child is playing.)
