**Expanded Basics:**

* **Small Talk (20 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Explain the importance of small talk in social interactions.
    * **Common Topics:**
        * Das Wetter (The weather): Wie ist das Wetter heute? (What's the weather like today?), Es regnet. (It's raining.), Die Sonne scheint. (The sun is shining.)
        * Hobbys (Hobbies): Was sind deine Hobbys? (What are your hobbies?), Ich spiele gern Fußball. (I like to play soccer.), Ich lese gern Bücher. (I like to read books.)
        * Beruf/Studium (Work/Studies): Was machst du beruflich? (What do you do for work?), Ich bin Student/in. (I am a student.)
        * Familie (Family): Hast du Geschwister? (Do you have siblings?), Ich habe eine Schwester. (I have a sister.)
        * Reisen (Travel): Bist du gern unterwegs? (Do you like to travel?), Ich war letztes Jahr in Italien. (I was in Italy last year.)
    * **Useful Phrases:**
        * Schön, Sie/dich kennenzulernen. (Nice to meet you.)
        * Freut mich auch. (Nice to meet you too.)
        * Wie geht es Ihnen/dir? (How are you?)
        * Danke, gut. Und Ihnen/dir? (Thanks, fine. And you?)
        * Entschuldigung... (Excuse me...)
    * **Practice Activities:** Role-playing simple small talk scenarios, filling in dialogues, answering questions about common small talk topics.

* **Asking for Information (25 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Explain how to ask for different types of information politely.
    * **Question Words:**
        * Wer? (Who?)
        * Was? (What?)
        * Wann? (When?)
        * Wo? (Where?)
        * Warum? (Why?)
        * Wie? (How?)
        * Wie viel/viele? (How much/many?)
        * Welche/r/s? (Which?)
    * **Asking for Directions:** Wo ist...? (Where is...?), Können Sie mir bitte sagen, wie ich zum Bahnhof komme? (Can you please tell me how to get to the train station?)
    * **Asking about Time:** Wie spät ist es? (What time is it?), Um wie viel Uhr...? (At what time...?)
    * **Asking about Prices:** Was kostet das? (How much does that cost?), Wie viel kostet...? (How much does... cost?)
    * **Asking for Clarification:** Können Sie das bitte wiederholen? (Can you please repeat that?), Langsamer bitte. (Slower, please.)
    * **Practice Activities:** Creating questions based on given answers, completing dialogues where someone is asking for information, role-playing asking for directions or prices.

* **Expressing Opinions (20 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Teach phrases for expressing agreement, disagreement, likes, and dislikes.
    * **Expressing Likes:** Ich mag... (I like...), Ich finde... toll/super/gut. (I find... great/super/good.), Mir gefällt... (I like...).
    * **Expressing Dislikes:** Ich mag... nicht. (I don't like...), Ich finde... schlecht/nicht gut. (I find... bad/not good.), Mir gefällt... nicht. (I don't like...).
    * **Expressing Agreement:** Ich stimme zu. (I agree.), Das finde ich auch. (I think so too.)
    * **Expressing Disagreement:** Ich stimme nicht zu. (I don't agree.), Das finde ich nicht. (I don't think so.), Ich sehe das anders. (I see it differently.)
    * **Giving Simple Opinions:** Ich denke, dass... (I think that...), Meiner Meinung nach... (In my opinion...).
    * **Using Adjectives:** Explain how to use adjectives to express opinions (e.g., Das Buch ist interessant. - The book is interesting.).
    * **Practice Activities:** Completing sentences to express opinions, responding to statements with agreement or disagreement, discussing simple topics and giving opinions.

* **Telling Stories (25 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Introduce how to talk about past events using the *Perfekt* tense (which will be covered in the grammar section).
    * **Basic Sequencing Words:** Zuerst (First), Dann (Then), Später (Later), Danach (After that), Zum Schluss (Finally).
    * **Simple Story Structures:** Starting with when and where something happened, describing the event, and giving a brief conclusion.
    * **Example Story Starters:** Letztes Wochenende... (Last weekend...), Gestern Abend... (Yesterday evening...), Einmal habe ich... (Once I...).
    * **Practice Activities:** Putting events in the correct order, completing simple stories, telling short stories based on prompts or pictures.

**Grammar:**

* **Past Tense (Perfekt) (30 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Explain the usage of the *Perfekt* tense for talking about completed actions in the past.
    * **Formation with *haben*:** Explain when to use *haben* as the auxiliary verb (most transitive verbs and some intransitive verbs). Show the conjugation of *haben* in the present tense. Explain the formation of the past participle (usually ge- + stem + -t). Provide examples:
        * Ich *habe* ein Buch *gelesen*. (I have read a book.)
        * Du *hast* Fußball *gespielt*. (You have played soccer.)
    * **Formation with *sein*:** Explain when to use *sein* as the auxiliary verb (intransitive verbs of movement and change of state, and the verb *sein* itself). Show the conjugation of *sein* in the present tense. Explain the formation of the past participle. Provide examples:
        * Ich *bin* nach Hause *gegangen*. (I have gone home.)
        * Du *bist* müde *geworden*. (You have become tired.)
        * Er *ist* in Berlin *gewesen*. (He has been in Berlin.)
    * **Irregular Past Participles:** Introduce some common irregular past participles (e.g., gewesen, geschrieben, gegessen, getrunken).
    * **Word Order:** Explain that the auxiliary verb (*haben* or *sein*) is in the second position, and the past participle is at the end of the sentence.
    * **Practice Activities:** Forming the *Perfekt* of given verbs, filling in the blanks in sentences using the *Perfekt*, translating simple past tense sentences.

* **Modal Verbs (25 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Introduce the common modal verbs and their meanings.
    * **List of Modal Verbs and Meanings:**
        * können (can, to be able to)
        * müssen (must, to have to)
        * wollen (want to)
        * sollen (should, ought to)
        * dürfen (may, to be allowed to)
        * möchten (would like to)
    * **Conjugation:** Show the conjugation of each modal verb in the present tense (note the stem changes in the singular forms).
    * **Sentence Structure:** Explain that when a modal verb is used, it is conjugated in the second position, and the main verb goes to the end of the sentence in the infinitive form.
    * **Examples:**
        * Ich *kann* Deutsch *sprechen*. (I can speak German.)
        * Du *musst* jetzt *gehen*. (You must go now.)
        * Wir *wollen* ein Eis *essen*. (We want to eat an ice cream.)
    * **Practice Activities:** Filling in the blanks with the correct modal verb, forming sentences using modal verbs, translating sentences with modal verbs.

* **Prepositions (30 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Explain what prepositions are and their role in sentences.
    * **Prepositions with Accusative Case:** Introduce common prepositions that take the accusative case (durch - through, für - for, gegen - against, ohne - without, um - around). Provide examples.
    * **Prepositions with Dative Case:** Introduce common prepositions that take the dative case (aus - from, außer - except for, bei - at/near, mit - with, nach - after/to (cities/countries), seit - since, von - from, zu - to). Provide examples.
    * **Two-Way Prepositions (Accusative or Dative):** Introduce common prepositions that can take either the accusative (movement towards) or dative (location) case (an - on/at, auf - on, hinter - behind, in - in, neben - next to, über - above/over, unter - under, vor - in front of, zwischen - between). Explain the difference in meaning and provide examples.
    * **Practice Activities:** Filling in the blanks with the correct preposition, choosing the correct case for nouns following prepositions, translating sentences with prepositions.

* **Conjunctions (25 min):**
    * **Introduction:** Explain how conjunctions connect words, phrases, and clauses.
    * **Coordinating Conjunctions:** Introduce common coordinating conjunctions that connect grammatically equal parts of a sentence (und - and, aber - but, oder - or, denn - because, sondern - but rather). Explain that word order in the main clauses remains the same. Provide examples.
    * **Subordinating Conjunctions:** Introduce common subordinating conjunctions that introduce a subordinate clause (weil - because, dass - that, wenn - if/when, als - when (past), obwohl - although). Explain that the verb goes to the end of the subordinate clause. Provide examples.
    * **Practice Activities:** Combining sentences using conjunctions, identifying the type of conjunction used, translating sentences with conjunctions.

**Practice:**

* **Intermediate Listening (15 min):**
    * **Scenario:** A conversation between two friends making plans for the weekend, or someone ordering food at a restaurant.
    * **Task:** Listen to the conversation and answer more detailed questions about what they are saying, where they are going, or what they are ordering.

* **Intermediate Reading (20 min):**
    * **Simple Text:** A short article about a common topic (e.g., a day trip, a hobby), a simple blog post, or a short email.
    * **Task:** Read the text and answer comprehension questions that require understanding more than just basic facts. This could include inferring meaning or understanding the main idea.

* **Intermediate Writing (20 min):**
    * **Prompt:** Write a short paragraph (5-7 sentences) about your last weekend or a recent trip.
    * **Alternative Prompt:** Write a short email to a friend accepting or declining an invitation.

* **Conversation Practice (Unlimited):**
    * **Scenarios:** The chatbot can engage in more complex conversations about the topics covered in the A2 level, such as discussing hobbies, making plans, talking about past experiences using the *Perfekt* tense, or expressing opinions.
    * **Error Correction:** The chatbot should provide more detailed feedback on grammatical errors and suggest alternative phrasing.

* **Modal Verbs Quiz (15 questions):**
