**Health and Body (20 min):**

* **Introduction:** Knowing the German words for parts of the body and common health issues is very useful for everyday situations, whether you're talking about yourself or understanding others. In this lesson, you will learn some important vocabulary related to health and the body.

* **Body Parts Vocabulary:**

    * **Head (Der Kopf):**
        * *das Haar* (hair)
        * *der Kopf* (head)
        * *das Gesicht* (face)
        * *die Stirn* (forehead)
        * *das Auge* (eye)
        * *die Augenbraue* (eyebrow)
        * *das Ohr* (ear)
        * *die Nase* (nose)
        * *der Mund* (mouth)
        * *die Lippe* (lip)
        * *die Zunge* (tongue)
        * *der Zahn* (tooth)
        * *das Kinn* (chin)
        * *die Wange* (cheek)

    * **Torso (Der Oberkörper):**
        * *der Hals* (neck)
        * *die Schulter* (shoulder)
        * *die Brust* (chest)
        * *der Bauch* (stomach)
        * *der Rücken* (back)

    * **Arms and Hands (Die Arme und Hände):**
        * *der Arm* (arm)
        * *der Ellenbogen* (elbow)
        * *das Handgelenk* (wrist)
        * *die Hand* (hand)
        * *der Finger* (finger)
        * *der Daumen* (thumb)

    * **Legs and Feet (Die Beine und Füße):**
        * *das Bein* (leg)
        * *das Knie* (knee)
        * *der Fuß* (foot)
        * *die Zehe* (toe)

    * **Internal Organs (Innere Organe) - Basic:**
        * *das Herz* (heart)
        * *der Magen* (stomach)

    * **Visual Aid:** Include a diagram of the human body with labels for each of these parts.

* **Common Illnesses Vocabulary:**

    * *die Erkältung* (cold)
    * *der Husten* (cough)
    * *der Schnupfen* (runny nose)
    * *das Fieber* (fever)
    * *die Grippe* (flu)
    * *der Kopfschmerz* (headache)
    * *der Bauchschmerz* (stomach ache)
    * *die Zahnschmerzen* (toothache)
    * *die Allergie* (allergy)
    * *die Verletzung* (injury)

* **Actions Related to Health:**

    * *sich fühlen* (to feel) - *Ich fühle mich gut/schlecht/krank.* (I feel good/bad/sick.)
    * *haben* (to have) - *Ich habe Kopfschmerzen.* (I have a headache.)
    * *weh tun* (to hurt) - *Mein Kopf tut weh.* (My head hurts.)
    * *husten* (to cough)
    * *niesen* (to sneeze)
    * *zum Arzt gehen* (to go to the doctor)
    * *Medizin nehmen* (to take medicine)
    * *sich ausruhen* (to rest)
    * *gesund sein* (to be healthy)
    * *krank sein* (to be sick)

* **Useful Phrases:**

    * *Was fehlt dir/Ihnen?* (What's wrong with you? - informal/formal)
    * *Ich fühle mich nicht gut.* (I don't feel well.)
    * *Ich habe...* (I have...) - followed by an illness.
    * *Wo tut es weh?* (Where does it hurt?)
    * *Ich brauche einen Arzt.* (I need a doctor.)
    * *Gute Besserung!* (Get well soon!)

* **Practice Activities:**

    1.  **Matching:** Match the German body part words to their corresponding pictures.
    2.  **Labeling:** Label the parts of a body diagram with the correct German words.
    3.  **Fill in the Blanks:** Complete sentences describing symptoms using the illness vocabulary.

        *Example:* Ich habe _______ und muss oft _______. (Ich habe Schnupfen und muss oft niesen.)

    4.  **Sentence Creation:** Create simple sentences using the "Actions Related to Health" vocabulary.

        *Example:* Ich _______, weil ich ______ habe. (Ich huste, weil ich eine Erkältung habe.)

    5.  **Dialogue Practice:** Read or listen to a short dialogue where someone is talking about their health and answer simple comprehension questions.

        *Example Dialogue:*
        > Person A: Hallo! Wie geht es dir?
        > Person B: Nicht so gut. Ich habe Kopfschmerzen und fühle mich müde.
        > Person A: Oh, das tut mir leid. Hast du Fieber?
        > Person B: Ich glaube nicht. Aber ich habe gestern zu lange gearbeitet.
        > Person A: Dann ruh dich gut aus! Gute Besserung!

        *Comprehension Question:* Was hat Person B? (Person B hat Kopfschmerzen und fühlt sich müde.)

* **Conclusion:** Briefly review the vocabulary learned in this lesson and encourage learners to use these words in everyday conversations.

Remember to include audio pronunciations for all the vocabulary words and phrases to help learners with their pronunciation. Visual aids are especially helpful for this topic.
