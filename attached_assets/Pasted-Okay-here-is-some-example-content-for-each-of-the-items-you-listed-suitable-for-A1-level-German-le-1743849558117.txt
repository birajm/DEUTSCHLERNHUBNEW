Okay, here is some example content for each of the items you listed, suitable for A1 level German learners:

Lessons:

Present Tense (30 min):

Introduction: Briefly explain what the present tense is and why it's important.
Regular Verb Conjugation:
Explain the verb stem and endings (-e, -st, -t, -en, -t, -en).
Provide examples with common regular verbs like machen (to make), spielen (to play), kaufen (to buy), wohnen (to live).
Show conjugation tables:
ich mache
du machst
er/sie/es macht
wir machen
ihr macht
Sie/sie machen
Common Irregular Verbs: Introduce the most common A1 irregular verbs with stem changes in the du/er/sie/es forms:
sein (to be): ich bin, du bist, er/sie/es ist, wir sind, ihr seid, Sie/sie sind
haben (to have): ich habe, du hast, er/sie/es hat, wir haben, ihr habt, Sie/sie haben   
werden (to become): ich werde, du wirst, er/sie/es wird, wir werden, ihr werdet, Sie/sie werden   
sprechen (to speak): ich spreche, du sprichst, er/sie/es spricht, wir sprechen, ihr sprecht, Sie/sie sprechen
essen (to eat): ich esse, du isst, er/sie/es isst, wir essen, ihr esst, Sie/sie essen
Practice Activities: Fill-in-the-blank exercises, matching verbs to subjects, conjugating given verbs.
Personal Pronouns (20 min):

Introduction: Explain what personal pronouns are and their function.
List of Pronouns:
ich (I)
du (you, informal, singular)
er (he)
sie (she)
es (it)
wir (we)
ihr (you, informal, plural)
Sie (you, formal, singular and plural)
sie (they)
Usage Examples: Provide simple sentences demonstrating the use of each pronoun.
Practice Activities: Matching pronouns to descriptions, filling in the blanks in sentences with the correct pronoun.
Simple Sentences (25 min):

Introduction: Explain the basic Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) structure in German.
Word Order: Emphasize that the verb usually comes in the second position in a declarative sentence.
Examples:
Ich bin Anna. (Subject + Verb + Complement)
Du sprichst Deutsch. (Subject + Verb + Object)
Er hat ein Buch. (Subject + Verb + Object)
Questions: Briefly introduce the word order in simple yes/no questions (Verb-Subject).
Bist du müde? (Verb + Subject + Complement)
Practice Activities: Rearranging words to form correct sentences, translating simple English sentences into German.

Practice:

Listening Exercise (10 min):

Scenario: A short audio clip of two people greeting each other and asking how they are.
Task: Listen to the audio and answer simple questions (e.g., What are their names? How are they feeling?).
Example Audio Script (very simple):
Person A: Hallo! Wie geht es dir?
Person B: Danke, gut. Und dir?
Person A: Auch gut, danke. Mein Name ist Lisa.
Person B: Schön, dich kennenzulernen, Lisa. Ich bin Tom.
Questions:
Wie heißt die Frau? (a) Tom, (b) Lisa
Wie geht es Tom? (a) Schlecht, (b) Gut
Reading Exercise (15 min):

Simple Text: "Ich bin Max. Ich wohne in Berlin. Ich bin 25 Jahre alt. Meine Lieblingsfarbe ist blau. Ich habe eine Schwester und einen Bruder. Meine Schwester heißt Anna und mein Bruder heißt Paul."
Task: Read the text and answer true/false questions or multiple-choice questions.
Example Questions:
Max wohnt in Hamburg. (True / False)
Wie alt ist Max? (a) 20, (b) 25, (c) 30
Hat Max Geschwister? (Ja / Nein)
Writing Exercise (15 min):

Prompt: Write three simple sentences about yourself. Include your name, where you live, and one thing you like.
Example Answer: Ich heiße [Your Name]. Ich wohne in [Your City/Town]. Ich mag [Something you like, e.g., Kaffee].
Alternative Prompt: Fill in the blanks to complete simple sentences (e.g., Ich ____ (sein) müde. Du ____ (haben) ein Buch.).
Chatbot Practice (Unlimited):

Scenarios: The chatbot can initiate conversations with basic greetings ("Hallo!", "Wie geht es dir?").
User Prompts: Encourage users to practice introducing themselves ("Ich bin..."), asking simple questions ("Wie heißt du?", "Was ist das?"), and expressing basic needs or preferences ("Ich möchte...").
Chatbot Responses: The chatbot should be programmed to understand and respond to these basic A1 level inputs, providing simple and grammatically correct answers. It can also offer gentle corrections if the user makes a mistake.
Quizzes:

Basic Phrases Quiz (10 questions):

Translate "Hello" into German.
What is the German for "Thank you"?
How do you say "Goodbye" in German?
Match the German phrase to its English translation (e.g., "Wie geht es Ihnen?" - "How are you? (formal)").
Fill in the blank: Guten ____ (Morning).
Articles Quiz (15 questions):

Choose the correct article for the following nouns: ____ Tisch, ____ Buch, ____ Lampe.
Match the noun to its correct gender (der, die, das).
Select the correct sentence using the appropriate article.
Present Tense Quiz (15 questions):

Conjugate the verb sprechen for "du".
Fill in the blank with the correct form of sein: Ich ____ müde.
Choose the correct conjugated form of the verb in a sentence (e.g., Er ____ (spielen) Fußball.).

Mix of questions covering vocabulary (from all topics), grammar (present tense, personal pronouns, articles), basic sentence structure, and simple comprehension (short texts or dialogues).
Question types can include multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank, matching, and true/false.
